The Fastest Way to Do Keyword Research Without Paid Tools

Keyword research has a reputation for being expensive, technical, and time-consuming. That reputation belongs to a different era. You do not need a $100-a-month subscription to find keywords worth ranking for.

Here is the fastest free method available right now — and it fits in a single prompt.

Why Bloggers Skip Keyword Research

The barrier has always been tools. Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz — powerful platforms with steep learning curves and steeper prices. Most bloggers, especially those just starting, cannot justify the cost before they have an audience. So they skip keyword research entirely and publish into silence.

AI has removed that barrier completely.

The Free AI Method

You already have access to ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Any of them will do. Here is the prompt that replaces a paid keyword tool for most everyday research needs:

Act as an SEO strategist. I need keyword research for "{your topic}" without any paid tools.

Give me:
- 10 long-tail keywords (4+ words) with low competition
- 10 question-based keywords people are actively searching
- 5 keywords with high buying intent

For each keyword: estimated monthly searches, competition level (low/medium/high), and one content angle.

That single prompt gives you 25 keywords across three categories — informational, question-based, and commercial. Most paid tools give you the same categories with more data but not necessarily better starting points.

What Free Tools to Pair With AI

Once you have your AI-generated list, validate the most promising keywords with these free tools:

Google Search Console — shows you what your existing pages already rank for and what queries bring real clicks.

Google autocomplete — type your keyword into Google and see what it suggests. Every suggestion is a real search people make.

Answer the Public (free tier) — generates question-based keywords around any topic.

None of these cost anything. Together with AI, they give you a complete keyword research workflow at zero cost.

How Fast Is This Actually?

From opening ChatGPT to having a validated keyword list: under ten minutes. That includes running the prompt, reviewing the output, and checking two or three terms in Google autocomplete. Most paid tool workflows take longer.

The One Thing Paid Tools Still Do Better

Exact search volume data. AI estimates are directionally useful but not precise. If you are making high-stakes content decisions, a paid tool's hard numbers are worth having. For most bloggers publishing one to three posts a week, AI estimates are accurate enough to work from.
